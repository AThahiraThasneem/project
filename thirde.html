Making delicious éclairs is a delightful pastry-making adventure. Here's a basic recipe for classic éclairs:

**Ingredients:**

**For the Choux Pastry:**

- 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
- 1 cup water
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 4 large eggs
- A pinch of salt
- A pinch of salt

**For the Filling (Pastry Cream):**

- 2 cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 4 large egg yolks
- 1/4 cup cornstarch
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

**For the Chocolate Glaze:**

- 4 oz semisweet chocolate, chopped
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

**Instructions:**

**1. Make the Choux Pastry:**

a. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
b. In a saucepan, combine the butter, water, and a pinch of salt. Heat over medium-high heat until the butter melts and the mixture boils.
c. Remove from heat and add the flour all at once. Stir vigorously until the mixture forms a smooth, thick dough.
d. Let the dough cool for a few minutes, then add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition, until you have a smooth, glossy dough.
e. Transfer the dough into a piping bag fitted with a large round tip and pipe éclair shapes onto the prepared baking sheet.
f. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 375°F (190°C) and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the éclairs are golden brown and puffed up. Remove from the oven and let them cool completely.

**2. Prepare the Pastry Cream:**

a. In a saucepan, heat the milk until it's just about to boil. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch until well combined.
b. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and comes to a boil.
c. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
d. Transfer the pastry cream to a bowl and cover it with plastic wrap directly touching the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Let it cool to room temperature.

**3. Fill the Éclairs:**

a. Once the éclairs have cooled, use a knife to make a small slit on one side.
b. Fill the éclairs with the pastry cream using a piping bag or a spoon.

**4. Make the Chocolate Glaze:**

a. In a heatproof bowl, combine the chopped chocolate and butter.
b. Heat the cream in a small saucepan until it's hot but not boiling.
c. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit for a minute, then stir until the chocolate is completely melted and the glaze is smooth.

**5. Glaze the Éclairs:**

a. Dip the tops of the filled éclairs into the chocolate glaze or drizzle it over the éclairs.
b. Place the éclairs on a wire rack to allow the glaze to set.
Enjoy your homemade éclairs! You can get creative with the filling and glaze by using different flavors or decorations to make them your own.
