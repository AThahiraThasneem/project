Here's a recipe to make traditional custard tarts:

**Ingredients:**

**For the Tart Crust:**
- 1 sheet of frozen puff pastry, thawed (or make your own pastry)

**For the Custard Filling:**
- 2 cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 4 large egg yolks
- 1/4 cup cornstarch
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- Ground nutmeg or cinnamon (optional, for dusting)

**Instructions:**
**1. Preheat the Oven:**
a. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
**2. Prepare the Tart Crust:**
a. Roll out the thawed puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. You can also make your own pastry crust if you prefer.
b. Using a round cutter or a glass that's slightly larger than your tart molds, cut out circles of pastry to line the tart molds.
c. Carefully line the tart molds with the pastry circles, pressing them gently into the molds. Trim any excess pastry.
d. Prick the bottom of the pastry with a fork to prevent it from puffing up too much during baking.

**3. Prepare the Custard Filling:**

a. In a saucepan, heat the milk until it's almost boiling. Remove it from the heat and set it aside.
b. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, egg yolks, and cornstarch until well combined.
c. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
d. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and comes to a boil.
e. Once the custard is thickened, remove it from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

**4. Fill the Tart Shells:**

a. Carefully pour the hot custard into the prepared tart shells.

**5. Bake:**

a. Place the filled tart shells in the preheated oven and bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the custard is set and the pastry is golden brown.

**6. Cool and Serve:**

a. Allow the custard tarts to cool in the tart molds for a few minutes. You can then remove them from the molds and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
b. Once they have cooled, you can dust the tops with ground nutmeg or cinnamon if you like.

**7. Serve:**

a. Custard tarts are best served at room temperature.

Enjoy your homemade custard tarts, which are wonderfully creamy and full of flavor. They make for a delightful dessert or sweet treat to accompany coffee or tea.
