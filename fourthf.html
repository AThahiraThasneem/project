Fruit cobblers are rustic, comforting desserts that are easy to make and can be adapted to your favorite fruits. Here's a basic recipe for a fruit cobbler:
**Ingredients:**
**For the Fruit Filling:**
- 4-5 cups of fresh or frozen fruit (e.g., peaches, berries, apples, cherries, etc.)
- 1/2 to 1 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste depending on fruit sweetness)
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch or tapioca starch (to thicken the filling)
**For the Cobbler Topping:**
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, melted
- 1/2 cup milk
**Instructions:**
**1. Preheat the Oven:**
- Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
**2. Prepare the Fruit Filling:**
a. In a large mixing bowl, combine the fruit with sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla extract (if using). Toss well to coat the fruit evenly.
b. Sprinkle the cornstarch or tapioca starch over the fruit mixture and stir to combine. This will help thicken the fruit juices as the cobbler bakes.
**3. Transfer the Fruit Filling:**
a. Pour the prepared fruit filling into a 9x13-inch baking dish or a similar-sized ovenproof dish.
**4. Prepare the Cobbler Topping:**
a. In another mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
b. Add the melted butter and milk to the dry ingredients. Stir until just combined; it will form a thick batter.
**5. Add the Cobbler Topping:**
a. Spoon the cobbler topping over the fruit filling in the baking dish. You can spread it evenly or leave it rustic-looking by dolloping spoonfuls over the fruit.
**6. Bake:**
a. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 40-50 minutes, or until the cobbler topping is golden brown and the fruit filling is bubbling.
**7. Cool Slightly and Serve:**
a. Allow the cobbler to cool slightly before serving. It's best enjoyed warm.
**8. Serving:*
* a. Serve your fruit cobbler as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, a dollop of whipped cream, or a drizzle of caramel sauce for extra indulgence.
Feel free to customize your cobbler by mixing and matching fruits or adding spices like cinnamon or nutmeg to the filling. Cobblers are a great way to showcase seasonal fruits and are perfect for casual gatherings and family desserts. Enjoy!
