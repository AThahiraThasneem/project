Recipe: Red Forest Cake

Preparation Time: 2 hours
Cooking Time: 30 minutes
Servings: 10-12 slices

Ingredients:

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
- ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¾ cup buttermilk
- ½ cup vegetable oil
- 2 large eggs
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup boiling water

For the filling and frosting:

- 2 cups heavy cream
- ¼ cup powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted and halved
- ½ cup cherry jam

For the garnish:

- Fresh cherries, for decoration
- Chocolate shavings, for decoration

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk together until well combined.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing well after each addition.
4. Slowly pour in the boiling water while continuing to mix. The batter will be thin, but that's normal. Stir until the batter is smooth.
5. Divide the batter equally between the prepared cake pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.
7. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
8. In the meantime, prepare the filling and frosting. In a large mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
9. Reserve about 1 cup of whipped cream for frosting the cake, and set it aside in the refrigerator.
10. Gently fold in the cherry jam into the remaining whipped cream until well combined. This will be the filling for the cake.
11. Once the cakes have cooled, slice each cake horizontally into two equal layers. You will have a total of four layers.
12. Place one layer on a serving plate and spread a thin layer of cherry whipped cream filling on top. Arrange a layer of cherry halves on top of the filling.
13. Repeat step 12 with the remaining layers, placing a layer of cake, then cherry filling, then cherries, until you reach the final layer.
14. Take the reserved whipped cream out of the refrigerator and frost the top and sides of the cake.
15. Decorate the top of the cake with fresh cherries and chocolate shavings.
16. Refrigerate the Red Forest Cake for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Note: You can also add a drizzle of chocolate ganache between the layers for added richness and depth of flavor.
