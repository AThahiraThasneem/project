Ingredients: -1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter
-2 cups granulated sugar
-4 large eggs
-1 teaspoon vanilla extract
-1 cup all-purpose flour
-2/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
-1/2 teaspoon salt
-1 cup chopped walnuts
-1 cup semisweet chocolate chips (optional, for extra fudginess)
Instructions:
1.Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch (23x33 cm) baking pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving some overhang for easy removal.
2.Melt the Butter: In a microwave-safe bowl or using a double boiler, melt the butter. Allow it to cool slightly.
3.Mix the Wet Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the melted butter and granulated sugar. Stir until well combined. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and mix until the batter is smooth.
4.Combine Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet mixture and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
5.Add Walnuts and Chocolate Chips: Gently fold in the chopped walnuts and chocolate chips (if you're using them). This will add texture and extra fudginess to your brownies.
6.Bake: Pour the brownie batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for about 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick or a cake tester inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs (but not wet batter). Baking times may vary, so keep a close eye on them.
7.Cool and Cut: Remove the brownies from the oven and allow them to cool in the pan for a while. Then, using the parchment paper overhang, lift the brownies out of the pan and place them on a wire rack to cool completely. This helps them set and develop that fudgy texture.
8.Slice and Serve: Once cooled, cut the brownies into squares or rectangles. Enjoy your homemade fudge walnut brownies!
9.You can store these brownies in an airtight container at room temperature for a few days. They are delicious on their own or with a scoop of ice cream. Feel free to adjust the recipe to your liking, and if you're a fan of extra fudginess, you can reduce the baking time slightly.
