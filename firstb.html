Recipe: Black Forest Cake

Ingredients:

- 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 cups granulated sugar
- 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
- 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup boiling water
- 1 can of pitted cherries (14 oz), drained (reserve the juice)
- 2 cups heavy whipping cream
- 1/4 cup powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- Shaved dark chocolate, for garnish

Instructions:

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans.
2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.
3. Add eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat on medium speed until well combined.
4. Stir in the boiling water, which will make the batter thin. Mix until the batter is smooth, without any lumps.
5. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared cake pans. Bake in the preheated oven for about 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
6. Remove the cakes from the oven and let them cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then, transfer the cakes onto a wire rack and let them cool completely.
7. In the meantime, prepare the whipped cream by pouring the heavy cream into a mixing bowl. Beat the cream with an electric mixer until soft peaks form. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and beat until stiff peaks form. Set aside.
8. Once the cakes have cooled, level the tops if necessary. Cut each cake in half horizontally to create four layers.
9. Place one layer of cake on a serving plate. Brush the top with some reserved cherry juice, then spread a layer of whipped cream on top. Scatter some drained cherries over the cream.
10. Repeat this process with the remaining layers, ending with a layer of whipped cream and cherries on top.
11. Decorate the sides of the cake with the remaining whipped cream using a spatula or piping bag for a professional finish.
12. Sprinkle the shaved dark chocolate over the top of the cake as a garnish.
13. Refrigerate the cake for at least 1 hour, or until ready to serve.
14. Slice and enjoy your homemade Black Forest Cake!

Note: Feel free to add an extra layer of whipped cream and cherries between each cake layer if you'd like a bolder cherry flavor.
