Making a fruit pie is a delightful way to enjoy seasonal fruits. Here's a basic recipe for a fruit pie, such as an apple or berry pie. You can adapt the filling to your favorite fruits.
**Ingredients:**
**For the Pie Crust:**
- 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
- 6-8 tablespoons ice-cold water

**For the Fruit Filling:**
- 6-8 cups of fresh or frozen fruit (e.g., apples, berries, peaches, etc.)
- 1 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste depending on fruit sweetness)
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch or tapioca starch
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (adjust to taste)
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (for dotting)

**Instructions:**

**1. Prepare the Pie Crust:**
a. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter.
b. Use a pastry cutter, fork, or your hands to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces of butter.
c. Gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix just until the dough starts to come together. You may not need all the water.
d. Divide the dough into two equal portions, shape each into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
**2. Prepare the Fruit Filling:**
a. If using apples or other firm fruits, peel and slice them. If using berries or softer fruits, you can leave them whole or slice them as needed.
b. In a large bowl, combine the fruit with sugar, cornstarch, ground cinnamon, and lemon juice. Toss well to coat the fruit evenly.
**3. Preheat the Oven:**
a. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
**4. Roll Out the Pie Crust:**
a. On a floured surface, roll out one of the pie crust disks into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter. This will be your bottom crust.
**5. Line the Pie Plate:**
a. Carefully transfer the rolled-out crust to a 9-inch pie plate. Press it gently into the plate, leaving any excess dough hanging over the edges.
**6. Add the Fruit Filling:**
a. Pour the prepared fruit filling into the pie crust in the pie plate. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter.
**7. Roll Out the Top Crust:**
a. Roll out the second pie crust disk into a similar-sized circle.
**8. Cover the Pie:**
a. Place the rolled-out top crust over the fruit filling. Trim any excess overhanging dough, leaving a small border. You can crimp the edges to seal the pie.
b. Make small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
**9. Bake:**
a. Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 15 minutes at 425°F (220°C).
b. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 35-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
**10. Cool and Serve:**
a. Allow the pie to cool on a wire rack for at least a couple of hours before slicing. This will allow the filling to set.
Serve your fruit pie with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a delicious, classic dessert. Enjoy!
